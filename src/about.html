About

Who are we?

>Women Hack for Non Profits (WHFNP) is a community run by women for women to get more ladies into coding and contributing to Open Source projects. By volunteering with Women Hack for Non-Profits, women can practice and grow the skills they require to help switch careers into the engineering sector, while working on real life projects and communicating with clients. As a group we help each other grow, boost self-confidence, share and exchange experiences, knowledge and best practices, and provide guidance at all levels.

We are over 300 on Slack and over 1000 on Meetup. Volunteers are a mixture of coders, designers, writers, project managers and testers working on projects that are personally rewarding and contribute to the society at large. Want a chance to work on real projects for non-profits and charities? Join us!

Our Founding Team

Established in 2015 Women Hack For Non-Profits (WHFNP) is a fast growing community of women that are looking to improve their coding, testing or design skills. What started out as a simple idea back in May 2015 by co-founders Nandhini Narasimhan and Vinita Rathi has been brought to life and grown to the wonderful community we have today.
